# Avan Makerspace Website Content Overhaul

## Overview
Comprehensive rewrite of the Avan Makerspace website to reflect a world-class, professional nonprofit brand suitable for international donors, partners, volunteers, schools, NGOs, governments, and the general public.

## Key Brand Updates

### Identity Enhancement
- **Refugee-Led & Queer-Led**: Prominently positioned throughout messaging
- **Location**: Kakuma Refugee Camp, Turkana County, Kenya
- **Tone**: Modern, inspiring, international, inclusive, human-centered
- **Focus**: Refugee innovation, creativity, dignity, belonging, hope

## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Do's
✓ Use active, empowering language
✓ Center youth agency and leadership
✓ Celebrate refugee identity
✓ Emphasize practical outcomes (jobs, income, skills)
✓ Tell human stories
✓ Be specific about programs and impact

### Don'ts
✗ Use patronizing language about refugees
✗ Center donors/outsiders as saviors
✗ Use generic nonprofit clichés
✗ Make promises without evidence
✗ Ignore systemic barriers
✗ Separate programs unnecessarily

## Conclusion

The redesigned Avan Makerspace website now positions the organization as a world-class, refugee-led innovation hub with credible, specific programming for international audiences. The messaging reflects the organization's authentic identity while clearly communicating impact for potential donors, partners, and supporters worldwide.
