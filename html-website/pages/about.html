About Avan

Meet the Team

Avan Makerspace is a refugee-led, queer-led nonprofit creating safe, inclusive spaces where displaced youth in Kakuma discover talents, develop digital and creative skills, and build sustainable futures.

Our Story

Founded by Refugees, for Refugees

Avan was founded to address a critical gap in Kakuma Refugee Camp: youth had dreams and potential, but no safe spaces or pathways to develop technology, creativity, and entrepreneurial skills. We started with a simple question: "What if we created spaces where displaced youth could lead innovation in their own community?"

Today, Avan is led by refugees, serves refugees and host-community youth, and operates on the belief that innovation knows no zip code—and that the most powerful leaders are those closest to the problems they solve.

Our Mission

Empowering Displaced Youth

We equip refugee and host-community youth with creative, digital, entrepreneurial, and leadership skills to transform their own lives and communities. We believe displaced youth are not problems to be solved—they are innovators, creators, and leaders ready to shape the future.

Our Values

Community-Led, Youth-Centered

  • Refugee-Led: Decisions made by those with lived experience
  • Queer-Affirming: Safe spaces for LGBTQ+ youth
  • Inclusive: 60% women and girls in all programs
  • Dignified: Respecting agency and choice
  • Innovative: Using technology and creativity as tools
