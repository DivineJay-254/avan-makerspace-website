About Avan Makerspace

Our Story

Avan Makerspace was founded with a simple yet powerful vision: to create safe, inclusive spaces where displaced youth in Kakuma Refugee Camp can discover their talents, develop essential skills, and build sustainable futures for themselves and their communities.

As a queer-led and refugee-led organization, we understand the unique challenges and strengths of the communities we serve. We believe that young people deserve not just survival, but the opportunity to thrive.

Our Values

Inclusion

We center marginalized voices and create spaces where everyone belongs.

Innovation

We encourage creative thinking and experimentation to solve real-world problems.

Community

We believe in the power of collective action and peer support.

Empowerment

We equip young people with tools to take control of their futures.

Sustainability

We focus on long-term, scalable solutions that create lasting change.

Integrity

We are transparent, accountable, and committed to ethical practices.

Leadership & Team

Avan Makerspace is led by a diverse team of passionate individuals from the refugee community and allied partners. Our leadership reflects the communities we serve, ensuring that decision-making remains grounded in lived experience and community wisdom.
